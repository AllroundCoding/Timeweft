# 06 · Resources, Economy & Trade  🟡 designed (carrying capacity built as a fixed value)

## Stockpiles at every scale

Resource buffers held by **individuals, households, and settlements** (food, water, gems,
money). Scale-polymorphic state — the same "stockpile" idea at every level. Disease, famine,
and war draw them down.

## Production & consumption → carrying capacity

Carrying capacity stops being a fixed number and becomes computed:

`K ≈ production (environment × labor × tech/institutions) − consumption`, per resource.

Environment bends it (Sandstorm cuts yield; the Mirage Coast grows year-round — canon).
This is what makes `K` *dynamic* instead of the placeholder `22`. The population engine
([05](05-population-and-emergence.md)) never changes — only how `K` is derived.

## Supply & demand → prices → specialization

Local scarcity sets prices; regions specialize by environment (Scorching Mountains → gems,
Mirage Coast → salt/fish, oases → grain) and trade their surpluses.

## Trade raises *effective* carrying capacity

A settlement exceeds its **local** `K` by importing carrying capacity from elsewhere — that
is *why a desert city like Azrak can exist*: caravans haul in what the oasis can't grow.
Trade is **cooperation across edges** (inter-settlement cohesion — see
[07](07-cooperation-projects-institutions.md)), tying cities, trade, and institutions into
one machinery reaching across a resource gap.

## Money & economic behavior

Money is handled per **disposition** ([03](03-agents-traits-needs.md)) — spender vs saver,
generous-but-thrifty — with culture and faith tenets nudging it. Hoarding vs sharing changes
a settlement's resilience to shocks.

## Shocks

Disease, famine, and war are resource/population shocks that drive the boom-bust loop
([05](05-population-and-emergence.md)).
